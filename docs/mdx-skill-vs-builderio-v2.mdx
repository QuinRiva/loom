## Canvas spacing verification

<Callout tone="decision">
  **Do not treat the larger spacing values as a BuilderIO deviation.** They are deliberate and
  correct for our renderer.
</Callout>

The renderer uses `CANVAS_BOARD_SCALE = 0.5`, so board coordinates map to pixels at roughly two board units per rendered pixel. `<Artboard>` applies that transform only to its absolute position. The inner `<ScreenRead flow={false}>` still renders at the fixed surface preset width from `screen.tsx`: browser 900, desktop 840, panel 420, popover 360, mobile 300.

That means a browser frame consumes roughly 1800 board units, not BuilderIO's 700-ish board units. A gap of 2200 board units renders as 1100px between origins, leaving about 200px of visible gutter after the 900px frame. BuilderIO's 1100 board-unit browser spacing would render as 550px between origins here, causing about 350px of overlap.

## Final recommendation

The expanded skill can be treated as **BuilderIO-authoring-quality parity** after the two small example-number cleanups above. No renderer work is required for parity. Do not add the hosted layer, legacy kit-tree, rough.js sketching, `data-icon` replacement, skeleton states, or a looser Diagram/HtmlBlock surface as part of this parity pass; those are correct-by-design divergences or separate product calls.
